2. what are the duties of an Auditor with regard to internal Control.

1. Responsibility of Management

The internal control system is designed and established by management. It primarily involves the duty of management to establish internal control. The Auditor is entitled to rely upon safeguards and internal control instituted by the Management.

2. Need for Evaluation

The Auditor has to ascertain that transactions are executed according to the authorisation of Management. He is interested in ascertaining that all transactions are recorded properly and are adequately safeguarded.

3. Need for Assurance

The Auditor requires reasonable assurance that the accounting system is adequate. This includes assurance that all accounting information is recorded in a correct, proper, and timely manner.

4. Audit Planning and Programme

The Auditor should gain an understanding of the accounting and related internal controls to evaluate the operations of these controls.

5. Review of Internal Control System

The Auditor needs to review the internal control system established by his client. He should identify weaknesses and suggest improvements.

He should write a letter of weaknesses with the following features:

  • a) List arrears of weaknesses.
  • b) Offer suggestions for improvements.
  • c) Help the management to rectify the weaknesses.
  • d) Help the auditor to minimize his legal liability.

3. what are the duties of an Auditor with regard to internal Check.

  • Auditor's Duty In Respect of Internal Check
    • The auditor may depend on Test Check if the system of internal check is full proof. However, the auditor should keep in mind that his liability is not reduced under any circumstance.
    • In the organisation where there is no internal control and the clerk has full control over all the books of accounts, it would be better if he checks all the transactions.
    • In the organisation where there is a good system of internal check, the auditor may rely upon it to a greater extent and apply a few test checks.
    • During the course of examination, if he comes across any mistake, he should go into the details of it.

    SA 400 has made the following recommendations in this case:

  • Recommendations
    • Establishment of the system: Basically the management is responsible for establishment of the internal check system.
    • Reliability: The auditor should verify the reliability of the system of internal check.
    • Evaluation: The auditor should evaluate the system to ensure that the system prevents errors and frauds. It ensures that all transactions are properly recorded.
      • Understanding the system: The auditor should get full knowledge of the system by having discussion with managers and by studying the manuals.
      • Application of test: He should test check the application of the system.
      • Judgement: He should judge whether the system is proper.
    • Reporting: Under MAOCARO, the auditor has to report:
      • a) Whether a service company has reasonable system of recording receipts, issues and consumption of materials and stores and allocation of materials consumed to the relative jobs.
      • b) Whether a service company has a reasonable system of allocating man-hours utilised to the relative jobs.
      • c) Whether there is a reasonable system of authorisation at proper levels on issue of stores and allocation of stores and labour to jobs.

4. Advantages of internal Control.

The advantages of Internal control may be summarised as follows:

  1. To avoid inefficiency in business.
  2. To prevent frauds.
  3. To increase accuracy and reliability in all the aspects of the business.
  4. To measure the effectiveness of business policies.
  5. To safeguard the assets of the organisation by avoiding wastes.
  6. To evaluate performance of the employees.
  7. To aid in management planning.
  8. To ensure that all transactions are duly authorised.
  9. To ensure that all transactions are properly recorded.
  10. To ensure that books of accounts are complete and accurate.
  11. To ensure that final accounts are reliable and ready in time.

5. What are Features of internal control as regards Credit purchases.

The features of internal control regarding credit purchases include:

  • Authorization and Approval: All purchases should be authorized by a responsible person before placing an order.
  • Supplier Verification: Vendors should be vetted, and only approved suppliers should be used.
  • Purchase Order System: Purchases should be initiated through a formal purchase order (PO).
  • Segregation of Duties: Different employees should handle ordering, receiving, and payment processing.
  • Receiving and Inspection of Goods: Goods received should be checked against the purchase order and invoice.
  • Recording and Documentation: Proper records should be maintained for all transactions.
  • Payment Controls: Payments should be verified and authorized before processing.
  • Periodic Reconciliation: Supplier accounts should be regularly reconciled with company records.
  • Credit Terms Compliance: Purchases should follow agreed credit terms to avoid penalties.
  • Fraud Prevention & Detection: Internal audits and employee role rotation should be conducted to prevent fraud.

1. How will you Vouch Cash Sales.

  1. Internal Check: Examine the system of internal check.
  2. Daily Cash Summary: Test check the Salesman's Daily Cash summary with the carbon copies of cash memos and see that they are properly entered. Also, check the daily stock summary of selling units.
  3. Proper Entry: See that the total of daily cash sales as per Salesman's abstract is properly entered on the receipt side of the cash book. If they are deposited into the bank, check the same with the counterfoil of Pay-in-slip books and Bank Pass Books.
  4. Documents: Check the relevant documents viz. Cash memo, Delivery challan, Excise gate pass, octroi receipt. In case of export, check Bill of lading, custom duty receipt, Airway Bill, proof of delivery, bank advice, etc.
  5. Authority for Discount: See that discounts and allowances are properly authorized.
  6. Difference in Cash Memo and Cash Book: Dates of cash memos and the dates on which receipts are recorded in the cash book should be the same. Any difference should be investigated.
  7. Reconciliation: Test check the reconciliation between sales and stocks.
  8. Posting: Test check the postings from the cash book into the ledger.
  9. Sale of Fixed Assets: Ensure that the sale of fixed assets is not included in sales.
  10. Sales of Scrap: See that the sale of scrap is recorded separately.

4. How will you Vouch Cash Purchase.

  • Internal Control: Ensure that the system of internal control is adequate and reliable.
  • Documents: Vouch the entries for cash purchases with reference to the cash memos, Goods Inward Register, Delivery challan, Excise Gate Pass, Transporter's Bill, Octroi Receipt, and Stores records. Ensure that correct and proper entries are made in the cash book.
  • Authority: Verify that such purchases have been duly authorized and are related to business needs, not for personal use.
  • Recording of Discount: Where discounts are allowed, ensure that only the net amount is paid and recorded in the cash book.
  • Certification by Responsible Officer: If a duplicate cash memo is produced instead of the original, confirm that it is not used for a bogus entry. It should be properly authorized and certified as genuine by a responsible officer.
  • Account Head: Ensure that the correct account head is assigned, distinguishing between purchases of raw materials, stores, stationery, etc.
  • Posting: Test check the posting of material amounts in the general ledger and verify that each item is correctly recorded.

5. How will you Vouch Royalties Received.

  • Agreement: Review the royalty agreement and understand provisions regarding calculation of royalty, such as rate, mode of calculation, due date, TDS, etc.
  • Correspondence: Go through correspondence with the other parties.
  • Test Check: Verify the calculation of royalty, rate, mode of calculation, and tax deduction.
  • Receipts: Examine counterfoils or carbon copies of the receipts issued to the parties.
  • Statement of Royalty: Verify the Royalty statement received and ensure that the royalty is calculated as per contract terms.
  • Cash Book: Examine the cash book with reference to the covering letter from the publisher.
  • Bank Statement: Trace the receipt of royalty in the bank statement.
  • T.D.S. Certificate: Verify that tax has been deducted at source and the certificate has been issued.
  • F.C. Earning: Ensure that earnings in foreign currency are disclosed separately.
