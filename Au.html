Question Bank

1. How will you Vouch Cash Sales.

  1. Internal Check: Examine the system of internal check.
  2. Daily Cash Summary: Test check the Salesman's Daily Cash summary with the carbon copies of cash memos and see that they are properly entered. Also, check the daily stock summary of selling units.
  3. Proper Entry: See that the total of daily cash sales as per Salesman's abstract is properly entered on the receipt side of the cash book. If they are deposited into the bank, check the same with the counterfoil of Pay-in-slip books and Bank Pass Books.
  4. Documents: Check the relevant documents viz. Cash memo, Delivery challan, Excise gate pass, octroi receipt. In case of export, check Bill of lading, custom duty receipt, Airway Bill, proof of delivery, bank advice, etc.
  5. Authority for Discount: See that discounts and allowances are properly authorized.
  6. Difference in Cash Memo and Cash Book: Dates of cash memos and the dates on which receipts are recorded in the cash book should be the same. Any difference should be investigated.
  7. Reconciliation: Test check the reconciliation between sales and stocks.
  8. Posting: Test check the postings from the cash book into the ledger.
  9. Sale of Fixed Assets: Ensure that the sale of fixed assets is not included in sales.
  10. Sales of Scrap: See that the sale of scrap is recorded separately.

2. How will you Vouch Credit sales.

  1. Check the Sales Invoices for sequential numbering and authorization.
  2. Verify the Sales Order to ensure proper approval and accuracy.
  3. Cross-check the Delivery Challan to confirm the goods were dispatched.
  4. Review the Customer’s Ledger Account for accurate recording.
  5. Obtain Customer Confirmations for outstanding balances.
  6. Check Credit Approval Documents to ensure customer eligibility.
  7. Reconcile with the Sales Register & General Ledger.
  8. Verify Goods Return & Discounts to ensure proper adjustments.
  9. Check Tax Compliance for accurate tax calculations.
  10. Identify Fraud or Manipulation by analyzing sales trends and invoices.

3. How will you Vouch Credit Purchase.

  1. Examine the Purchase Order (PO) for proper authorization.
  2. Verify the Supplier’s Invoice for accuracy in amount and quantity.
  3. Cross-check the Goods Received Note (GRN) to confirm receipt.
  4. Review the Supplier’s Ledger Account for correct posting.
  5. Check Payment Terms & Approval for adherence to company policies.
  6. Verify Goods Return & Debit Notes for proper adjustments.
  7. Reconcile with the Purchase Register & General Ledger.
  8. Ensure Tax Compliance with applicable GST/VAT calculations.
  9. Confirm the Supplier’s Existence & Authenticity to avoid fraud.
  10. Identify Fraud or Errors by detecting duplicate purchases or inflated invoices.

4. How will you Vouch Cash Purchase.

  1. Internal Control: Ensure that the system of internal control is adequate and reliable.
  2. Documents: Vouch the entries for cash purchases with reference to the cash memos, Goods Inward Register, Delivery challan, Excise Gate Pass, Transporter's Bill, Octroi Receipt, and Stores records. Ensure that correct and proper entries are made in the cash book.
  3. Authority: Verify that such purchases have been duly authorized and are related to business needs, not for personal use.
  4. Recording of Discount: Where discounts are allowed, ensure that only the net amount is paid and recorded in the cash book.
  5. Certification by Responsible Officer: If a duplicate cash memo is produced instead of the original, confirm that it is not used for a bogus entry. It should be properly authorized and certified as genuine by a responsible officer.
  6. Account Head: Ensure that the correct account head is assigned, distinguishing between purchases of raw materials, stores, stationery, etc.
  7. Posting: Test check the posting of material amounts in the general ledger and verify that each item is correctly recorded.

5. How will you Vouch Royalties Received.

  1. Agreement: Review the royalty agreement and understand provisions regarding calculation of royalty, such as rate, mode of calculation, due date, TDS, etc.
  2. Correspondence: Go through correspondence with the other parties.
  3. Test Check: Verify the calculation of royalty, rate, mode of calculation, and tax deduction.
  4. Receipts: Examine counterfoils or carbon copies of the receipts issued to the parties.
  5. Statement of Royalty: Verify the Royalty statement received and ensure that the royalty is calculated as per contract terms.
