2. what are the duties of an Auditor with regard to internal Control.

1. Responsibility of Management

The internal control system is designed and established by management. It primarily involves the duty of management to establish internal control. The Auditor is entitled to rely upon safeguards and internal control instituted by the Management.

2. Need for Evaluation

The Auditor has to ascertain that transactions are executed according to the authorisation of Management. He is interested in ascertaining that all transactions are recorded properly and are adequately safeguarded.

3. Need for Assurance

The Auditor requires reasonable assurance that the accounting system is adequate. This includes assurance that all accounting information is recorded in a correct, proper, and timely manner.

4. Audit Planning and Programme

The Auditor should gain an understanding of the accounting and related internal controls to evaluate the operations of these controls.

5. Review of Internal Control System

The Auditor needs to review the internal control system established by his client. He should identify weaknesses and suggest improvements.

He should write a letter of weaknesses with the following features:

  • a) List arrears of weaknesses.
  • b) Offer suggestions for improvements.
  • c) Help the management to rectify the weaknesses.
  • d) Help the auditor to minimize his legal liability.
