1. Explain the techniques of Auditing.

These are the techniques by which an auditor obtains evidence about the transactions recorded in the books of accounts. There are various techniques of audit

  1. Inspection

    2. It consists of examination of records, documents and tangible assets. Examination of documents is one of the most important techniques of auditing. Authorisation refers to that there is a proper approval for the transaction.

  2. Observation

    3. The auditor may observe stock taking done by the staff.

    • Admission Procedure in College
    • Security Observation
    • Fuel Filling at Petrol Pumps
    • Examination Time - Observing Students Trying to Copy
    • Observing → Selection - Buying - Packing - Delivery - Goods in Departmental Stores/Mall

  3. Enquiry/Inquiry and Confirmation

    4. It amounts to seeking appropriate information from a right person i.e. a person who has got knowledge of the transactions. The auditor may make enquiry by asking oral questions to the concerned persons. Alternatively, he may prepare a questionnaire and send it to the right persons with a request to complete the same and send back to the auditor.

  4. Computaion

    5. It refers, to checking the arithmetical accuracy of the transactions recorded in the books of accounts. The auditor may follow the same procedures as is followed by the accountant for verifying arithmetical accuracy.

  5. Analytical Review

    6. It implies studying important ratios and trends and investigating into the unusual 2.3 fluctuations. While auditing financial statements the auditor can correlate various figures in 1. order to establish financial soundness of the company. During the course of audit the auditor can scan various unusual transactions and examine them thoroughly. Proper scanning of the transactions requires greater degree of skill on the part of the auditor.

2. what are the duties of an Auditor with regard to internal Control.

1. Responsibility of Management

The internal control system is designed and established by management. It primarily involves the duty of management to establish internal control. The Auditor is entitled to rely upon safeguards and internal control instituted by the Management.

2. Need for Evaluation

The Auditor has to ascertain that transactions are executed according to the authorisation of Management. He is interested in ascertaining that all transactions are recorded properly and are adequately safeguarded.

3. Need for Assurance

The Auditor requires reasonable assurance that the accounting system is adequate. This includes assurance that all accounting information is recorded in a correct, proper, and timely manner.

4. Audit Planning and Programme

The Auditor should gain an understanding of the accounting and related internal controls to evaluate the operations of these controls.

5. Review of Internal Control System

The Auditor needs to review the internal control system established by his client. He should identify weaknesses and suggest improvements.

He should write a letter of weaknesses with the following features:

  • a) List arrears of weaknesses.
  • b) Offer suggestions for improvements.
  • c) Help the management to rectify the weaknesses.
  • d) Help the auditor to minimize his legal liability.

3. what are the duties of an Auditor with regard to internal Check.

  • Auditor's Duty In Respect of Internal Check
    • The auditor may depend on Test Check if the system of internal check is full proof. However, the auditor should keep in mind that his liability is not reduced under any circumstance.
    • In the organisation where there is no internal control and the clerk has full control over all the books of accounts, it would be better if he checks all the transactions.
    • In the organisation where there is a good system of internal check, the auditor may rely upon it to a greater extent and apply a few test checks.
    • During the course of examination, if he comes across any mistake, he should go into the details of it.

    SA 400 has made the following recommendations in this case:

  • Recommendations
    • Establishment of the system: Basically the management is responsible for establishment of the internal check system.
    • Reliability: The auditor should verify the reliability of the system of internal check.
    • Evaluation: The auditor should evaluate the system to ensure that the system prevents errors and frauds. It ensures that all transactions are properly recorded.
      • Understanding the system: The auditor should get full knowledge of the system by having discussion with managers and by studying the manuals.
      • Application of test: He should test check the application of the system.
      • Judgement: He should judge whether the system is proper.
    • Reporting: Under MAOCARO, the auditor has to report:
      • a) Whether a service company has reasonable system of recording receipts, issues and consumption of materials and stores and allocation of materials consumed to the relative jobs.
      • b) Whether a service company has a reasonable system of allocating man-hours utilised to the relative jobs.
      • c) Whether there is a reasonable system of authorisation at proper levels on issue of stores and allocation of stores and labour to jobs.

4. Advantages of internal Control.

The advantages of Internal control may be summarised as follows:

  1. To avoid inefficiency in business.
  2. To prevent frauds.
  3. To increase accuracy and reliability in all the aspects of the business.
  4. To measure the effectiveness of business policies.
  5. To safeguard the assets of the organisation by avoiding wastes.
  6. To evaluate performance of the employees.
  7. To aid in management planning.
  8. To ensure that all transactions are duly authorised.
  9. To ensure that all transactions are properly recorded.
  10. To ensure that books of accounts are complete and accurate.
  11. To ensure that final accounts are reliable and ready in time.

5. What are Features of internal control as regards Credit purchases.

The features of internal control regarding credit purchases include:

  1. Authorization and Approval: All purchases should be authorized by a responsible person before placing an order.
  2. Supplier Verification: Vendors should be vetted, and only approved suppliers should be used.
  3. Purchase Order System: Purchases should be initiated through a formal purchase order (PO).
  4. Segregation of Duties: Different employees should handle ordering, receiving, and payment processing.
  5. Receiving and Inspection of Goods: Goods received should be checked against the purchase order and invoice.
  6. Recording and Documentation: Proper records should be maintained for all transactions.
  7. Payment Controls: Payments should be verified and authorized before processing.
  8. Periodic Reconciliation: Supplier accounts should be regularly reconciled with company records.
  9. Credit Terms Compliance: Purchases should follow agreed credit terms to avoid penalties.
  10. Fraud Prevention & Detection: Internal audits and employee role rotation should be conducted to prevent fraud.


7/8 Mark Question

1. How will you Vouch Cash Sales.

  1. Internal Check: Examine the system of internal check.
  2. Daily Cash Summary: Test check the Salesman's Daily Cash summary with the carbon copies of cash memos and see that they are properly entered. Also, check the daily stock summary of selling units.
  3. Proper Entry: See that the total of daily cash sales as per Salesman's abstract is properly entered on the receipt side of the cash book. If they are deposited into the bank, check the same with the counterfoil of Pay-in-slip books and Bank Pass Books.
  4. Documents: Check the relevant documents viz. Cash memo, Delivery challan, Excise gate pass, octroi receipt. In case of export, check Bill of lading, custom duty receipt, Airway Bill, proof of delivery, bank advice, etc.
  5. Authority for Discount: See that discounts and allowances are properly authorized.
  6. Difference in Cash Memo and Cash Book: Dates of cash memos and the dates on which receipts are recorded in the cash book should be the same. Any difference should be investigated.
  7. Reconciliation: Test check the reconciliation between sales and stocks.
  8. Posting: Test check the postings from the cash book into the ledger.
  9. Sale of Fixed Assets: Ensure that the sale of fixed assets is not included in sales.
  10. Sales of Scrap: See that the sale of scrap is recorded separately.

2. How will you Vouch Credit sales.

  1. Check the Sales Invoices for sequential numbering and authorization.
  2. Verify the Sales Order to ensure proper approval and accuracy.
  3. Cross-check the Delivery Challan to confirm the goods were dispatched.
  4. Review the Customer’s Ledger Account for accurate recording.
  5. Obtain Customer Confirmations for outstanding balances.
  6. Check Credit Approval Documents to ensure customer eligibility.
  7. Reconcile with the Sales Register & General Ledger.
  8. Verify Goods Return & Discounts to ensure proper adjustments.
  9. Check Tax Compliance for accurate tax calculations.
  10. Identify Fraud or Manipulation by analyzing sales trends and invoices.

3. How will you Vouch Credit Purchase.

  1. Examine the Purchase Order (PO) for proper authorization.
  2. Verify the Supplier’s Invoice for accuracy in amount and quantity.
  3. Cross-check the Goods Received Note (GRN) to confirm receipt.
  4. Review the Supplier’s Ledger Account for correct posting.
  5. Check Payment Terms & Approval for adherence to company policies.
  6. Verify Goods Return & Debit Notes for proper adjustments.
  7. Reconcile with the Purchase Register & General Ledger.
  8. Ensure Tax Compliance with applicable GST/VAT calculations.
  9. Confirm the Supplier’s Existence & Authenticity to avoid fraud.
  10. Identify Fraud or Errors by detecting duplicate purchases or inflated invoices.

4. How will you Vouch Cash Purchase.

  1. Internal Control: Ensure that the system of internal control is adequate and reliable.
  2. Documents: Vouch the entries for cash purchases with reference to the cash memos, Goods Inward Register, Delivery challan, Excise Gate Pass, Transporter's Bill, Octroi Receipt, and Stores records. Ensure that correct and proper entries are made in the cash book.
  3. Authority: Verify that such purchases have been duly authorized and are related to business needs, not for personal use.
  4. Recording of Discount: Where discounts are allowed, ensure that only the net amount is paid and recorded in the cash book.
  5. Certification by Responsible Officer: If a duplicate cash memo is produced instead of the original, confirm that it is not used for a bogus entry. It should be properly authorized and certified as genuine by a responsible officer.
  6. Account Head: Ensure that the correct account head is assigned, distinguishing between purchases of raw materials, stores, stationery, etc.
  7. Posting: Test check the posting of material amounts in the general ledger and verify that each item is correctly recorded.

5. How will you Vouch Royalties Received.

  1. Agreement: Review the royalty agreement and understand provisions regarding calculation of royalty, such as rate, mode of calculation, due date, TDS, etc.
  2. Correspondence: Go through correspondence with the other parties.
  3. Test Check: Verify the calculation of royalty, rate, mode of calculation, and tax deduction.
  4. Receipts: Examine counterfoils or carbon copies of the receipts issued to the parties.
  5. Statement of Royalty: Verify the Royalty statement received and ensure that the royalty is calculated as per contract terms.
  6. Cash Book: Examine the cash book with reference to the covering letter from the publisher.
  7. Bank Statement: Trace the receipt of royalty in the bank statement.
  8. T.D.S. Certificate: Verify that tax has been deducted at source and the certificate has been issued.
  9. F.C. Earning: Ensure that earnings in foreign currency are disclosed separately.

6. How will you verify Fixed assets.

  1. Existence: The auditor should confirm that all the assets of the company are physically existing on the date of the Balance Sheet. It is possible that machinery is destroyed by an accident and scrapped but still appears in the books. On verification, the auditor can find out that it is not existing, and then it can be rectified.
  2. Possession: The auditor has to verify that the assets are in the possession of the company on the date of the Balance Sheet. For example, if an old machinery is sold for 65,000 but not recorded in the books of accounts, the auditor can check possession during verification and rectify the error.
  3. Ownership: The auditor should confirm that the asset is legally owned by the company. If a vehicle is in the name of an employee as per the Registration Book, it cannot be treated as a company asset. The invoice and entry in the Registration Book should be in the company's name to establish ownership rights.
  4. Charge or Lien: The auditor should verify whether the asset is subject to any charge or lien. The fact of the charge should be disclosed in financial statements.
  5. Record: The auditor should confirm that the assets and liabilities are recorded in the books and that there is no omission of any asset or liability. The auditor should ensure that no bogus asset or liability is recorded.

7. How will you verify Furniture & fixtures.

  1. Internal Control: Ensure a suitable internal control system exists for recording purchases and inspection of furniture & fixtures.
  2. Serial No: Ensure each item of furniture is given a stock register serial number, which is painted on the item.
  3. Schedule of Furniture: Obtain a schedule of furniture showing details such as location, cost, and acquisition date.
  4. Physical Examination: Conduct a physical examination to verify existence.
  5. Verify Cost: Cross-check the cost of furniture with invoices and ensure any purchase expenditures are capitalized.
  6. Depreciation: Verify that adequate depreciation is provided for furniture and fixtures.
  7. Verify Existence: Observe the physical verification being conducted by the management.
  8. Verify Ownership: Examine purchase invoices, freight bills, insurance, and installation charges. Check loan documents to verify if the furniture is mortgaged.
  9. Verify Disclosures: Ensure compliance with AS10, Schedule III of the Companies Act 2013, and CARO 2020. Verify disclosure in the Balance Sheet under "Property, Plant & Equipment."

8. How you verify Plant & Machinery

1) Review of Internal Control
The Auditor should review the system of internal control regarding acquisition and the use of Plant & Machinery, especially related to capital expenditure, acquisition records, position, and use.

2) Ownership
The Auditor should ensure that the machinery is owned by the entity and should verify the relevant documents like purchase invoices and ownership certificates.

3) Scrutiny
The Auditor should scrutinize the ledger accounts to ascertain the nature and correctness of the transactions related to Plant & Machinery.

4) Physical Verification
The Auditor, if possible, should physically examine Plant & Machinery periodically, at least once in 3 or 5 years, to confirm their existence and condition.

5) Depreciation Check
The Auditor should verify whether depreciation is charged appropriately as per applicable accounting standards and policies.

6) Insurance Coverage
The Auditor should check if the machinery is adequately insured against risks like fire, theft, and natural calamities.

7) Capitalization Policy
The Auditor should review whether the organization is following a consistent capitalization policy in accounting for new machinery and related expenses.

8) Maintenance Records
The Auditor should review maintenance logs and servicing records to ensure proper upkeep and functioning of the machinery.
