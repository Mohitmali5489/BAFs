1. Define leadership and state its functions

Introduction: Leadership is the ability to inspire, motivate, and guide individuals or groups towards the achievement of goals. It plays a crucial role in both professional and personal life, shaping the direction and success of any organization or team.

Key Functions of Leadership:

  1. Visionary Thinking – A leader must have a clear vision and goals for the future.
  2. Effective Communication – Strong leaders express their ideas clearly and listen actively.
  3. Decision-Making Skills – Quick and rational decision-making is vital.
  4. Integrity and Honesty – Ethical behavior builds trust and credibility.
  5. Empathy – Understanding and considering team members’ emotions and perspectives.
  6. Adaptability – Adjusting to changes and challenges with a positive attitude.
  7. Confidence – A leader must be self-assured and inspire confidence in others.
  8. Delegation Skills – Assigning tasks effectively to team members.
  9. Problem-Solving Abilities – Addressing issues with creative solutions.
  10. Motivational Abilities – Encouraging and inspiring the team to achieve goals.

2. Write a detailed note on life skills recommended by the WHO.

The World Health Organization (WHO) has identified ten essential life skills that are important for individuals to lead a successful and balanced life. These are:

  • Self-awareness: Understanding one's strengths, weaknesses, emotions, and values.
  • Empathy: Understanding and sharing others' feelings.
  • Critical thinking: Objectively analyzing information and situations.
  • Creative thinking: Finding innovative solutions to problems.
  • Decision-making: Choosing the best option from alternatives.
  • Problem-solving: Identifying and resolving challenges effectively.
  • Effective communication: Expressing thoughts and emotions clearly.
  • Interpersonal relationships: Building and maintaining strong relationships.
  • Coping with stress: Managing life’s pressures positively.
  • Coping with emotions: Managing one's emotions to avoid impulsive actions.
